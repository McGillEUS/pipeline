SEAD Courses
Academic
Notice

TISED is now offering credited courses under the code ‘SEAD’ (Sustainability in Engineering And Design) in the Faculty of Engineering. We currently have two courses open for undergraduate registration: SEAD 510 Energy Analysis (Fall 2018) and SEAD 515 Climate Change Adaptation and Engineering Infrastructure (Winter 2019).

SEAD 510: Energy Analysis (previously ‘FACC 510’) – FALL 2018
Critical analysis of the importance of energy to society, the unsustainability of the current energy system, and potential options for a sustainable energy system. Topics include: peak oil and climate change, fundamental energy metrics, traditional and alternative primary and secondary power systems, and energy storage technologies. Quantitative energy analysis is applied to a set of case studies investigating energy use, energy generation, and energy storage and transport.
Click here for more info and registration.

SEAD 515: Climate Change Adaptation and Engineering Infrastructure – WINTER 2019
Climate resilience and sustainability of engineering systems such as the built environment and engineering infrastructure in the context of a changing climate, possible mitigation and adaptation strategies and associated challenges and opportunities. Review of the basic principles that underpin the science of climate change; the role of global and regional climate models in predicting the behaviour of the climate system in response to different forcing scenarios, and the use of climate model outputs in support of across scale climate-resilience of various engineering systems including infrastructure systems.
Click here for more info and registration.

Next 36 applications are now open!
October 16
Entrepreneurship
Application

Ready to kick-start your career as an entrepreneur? The Next 36 is a NEXT Canada program that delivers a world-class founder development program to the nation’s most promising young entrepreneurs. The program provides a select group of undergraduate students and recent grads with a unique combination of education, mentorship and up to $80,000 in seed capital to launch a new business.

Applicants need to be in the last two years of their degree, have graduated within the past two years or be a grad student. You don't need to already have a business or a team, you can start something brand new in the program. Applications are due October 16, 2018!

For more info check out the NEXT 36 website or contact the NEXT Canada Ambassador for Montreal, Justin Hunt (McGill Mech Eng '18) at jhunt@blaisetransit.com
