Involvement Night

March 18
Involvement
Event

Want to get involved? Not sure in what, or how to go about it? Well, this event is for you!

Come out to Involvement Night on Monday, March 18th from 6-8PM to meet lots of people involved in all kinds of EUS groups, including departmental societies, committees, clubs, and design teams. You'll get a chance to talk about what involvement really is, its perks and drawbacks, and how to get started! If you're not sure what kind of groups you're interested in, there will also be a speed networking portion to help you find the best fit.

It will be an awesome first step into getting involved, regardless of what kind of involvement or how much of it you'd like to do! If you're interested, please fill out the participant registration form. Dinner will be provided at the event!

ACCE Team is recruiting volunteers!

March 11
Involvement
Application

What is the MOST essential skill for any student to actively learn and practice nowadays? You can likely come up with a long list and we are sure these skills all relate to LEADERSHIP! Don’t miss this great opportunity to accelerate your leadership development within a fantastic group of peers!

The ACCE Team consists of individuals passionate about leadership and who care about helping other students. The mission of ACCE is to encourage students to get involved on campus and/or in the Montreal community by building facilitation skills, developing leadership skills, and creating meaningful personal connections. Our vision is to develop and sustain a culture of empowerment that values experiential learning and creating learning organizations. More information is available on the ACCE website.

Applications for the ACCE Team are open until March 11th at 12 noon. If you have any questions, you can contact us at leadership.training@mcgill.ca.

McGill Startup Fair

March 19
Entrepreneurship
Event

Startups are the most exciting and rewarding thing you can dive into after graduation. They’re high-risk, and high-reward. Once people get a taste of the speed you can innovate at within a small company, it’s hard to go back to working for big companies with hulking bureaucracies that slow you down and never give you stock options.

But to break into this secret world, you need a guide. That’s why we’re inaugurating the first ever, McGill Startup Fair this winter: we’re going to make the startup world smaller and more navigable for you by bringing everybody you need to meet, in one place. You’ll get to meet with recruiters and founders from startups, venture capital firms, and companies that are at the forefront of innovation and impact.

Dress well, have a positive attitude and prepare good questions!

Find more information at our facebook event page: Facebook Event Page

 

McGill Dobson Center Startup Legals Workshop: Protecting Your IP & Incorporation

March 13
Entrepreneurship
Event

When building your startup it is very easy to make legal mistakes. It starts from day one, with not making the deal clear with your co-founder or not choosing the right type of business when incorporating. This topic will only get more complex as your company grows. If you are developing a new technology the discussion around how to protect your intellectual property is crucial.

The McGill Dobson Centre for Entrepreneurship and Compass Startup Legal Clinic are bringing together experts from the legal world - Dentons and Podlegal to demystify everything you should know to successfully launch your venture!

Find out more information on our Facebook event.
