SACOMSS’ Annual December 6th Vigil

December 6
Memorial
Event

On December 6, 1989, fourteen women were killed in the Ecole Polytechnique Massacre, one of the deadliest mass shootings in Canadian history, which was carried out in order to "fight feminists." December 6th has come to serve as a symbol for the pervasive gendered and sexual violence that these women faced, and many still face today. Join us on Thursday, December 6 in the Birks Chapel from 5pm to 7pm to come together as a community and stand in solidarity with all those who have, and still are, experiencing gendered and sexual violence in their daily lives. Food will be provided, in addition to a series of musical and spoken word poetry performances. If you would like to speak or perform at the memorial, please check out our Facebook Call-Out or email specialprojects@sacomms.org!

Apply for the Student Sponsorship Program Today!

December 1
Involvement
Application

Need funding for a project? The McGill Alumni Association and University Advancement provide financial support to student groups in the form of sponsorships. Ranging from $250 to $750, the objective of the Student Sponsorship Program is to financially support student groups for projects that do not otherwise receive University funding and that promote outside-the-classroom student experiences and/or student-alumni engagement opportunities.

For more information and to apply for the MAA Student Sponsorship Program, please complete the application form and send it to studentprograms.dev@mcgill.ca before December 1st.

McGill AI: Research Talks

Involvement
Application

If you’re an undergraduate or graduate student partaking in AI-related research (e.g. Computer Vision, ML, NLP, Ethics), this is your chance to shine!

We'll be hosting bi-weekly research talks - on campus - where you can present your research in front of a small audience. Interested?

1. Fill out this form to apply.
2. Our Graduate Student Representative, Joshua Durso-Finley, will get back to you with available time slots.
3. Show up and present. We'll handle all the logistics!

While we are open to students from all levels attending any university in Montreal, preference will be given to graduate students.

The setting for these talks will be informal and on an as-requested basis. Think of this as an opportunity to share what's going on in your lab, get feedback on your results, or encourage others to explore your field! You'll also become an official CONTRIBUTOR to the McGill AI Society.

Questions? Reach out to Joshua Durso-Finley with the subject line "McGill AI Research Talks" at joshua.durso-finley@mail.mcgill.ca.
