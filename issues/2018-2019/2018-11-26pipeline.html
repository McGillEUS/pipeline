Hult Prize at McGill

December 1
Entrepreneurship
Competition

The Hult Prize is a social entrepreneurship competition that happens yearly at McGill. It was invented to inspire people of the university level to change the world through business. Yearly, teams of 3 to 4 people compete with the opportunity to go to regionals and then possibly abroad. This year the competition is happening December 1, 2018 with location TBD. The challenge is to create jobs for 10,000 jobs for youth. To find team members please join the Facebook group: Hult Prize at McGill - Matchmaking and like the Facebook page Hult Prize at McGill - Competition for more updates! Deadline to sign up a team is November 20th!

TISED/GHP Innovative Solutions for Planetary Health Seed Grant Program Reception

November 19
Academic
Event

TISED is hosting a 5-a-7 reception highlighting the Innovative Solutions for Planetary Health Seed Grant program and its first grantees. the TISED/GHP Innovative Solutions for Planetary Health Seed Grants program provides seed funding for research at the interface of global health and sustainable development. Research projects are led by one researcher from the Faculty of Medicine and one researcher from the Faculty of Engineering. The program funds bold and high-risk research projects at the nexus between disease, rapid environmental degradation and climate change. The two grants on Water and Air pollution will be presented as follows:

High speed, portable PCR system for rapid and in-situ water quality testing
Co-PIs:
Dr. Andrew Kirk, Department of Electrical and Computer Engineering, Faculty of Engineering
Dr. Mark Trifiro, Department of Endocrinology in the Faculty of Medicine

A pilot study of the acute cardiovascular impacts of exposure to traffic-related air pollution and its oxidative potential among adults in urban Colombia
Co-PIs:
Dr. Jill Baumgartner, Department of Epidemiology, Biostatistics and Occupational Health in the Faculty of Medicine
Dr. Omid Rouhani, Department of Civil Engineering in the Faculty of Engineering

This will be followed by an expert panel discussion. It will be on Monday, November 19, 2018 5:00 PM to 7:00 PM at the Billiard Room, 3rd floor, McGill Faculty Club, 3450 McTavish St, Montreal. Register here: https://mcgill.ca/tised/events/20181119

McGill AI: Research Talks

Involvement
Application

If you’re an undergraduate or graduate student partaking in AI-related research (e.g. Computer Vision, ML, NLP, Ethics), this is your chance to shine!

We'll be hosting bi-weekly research talks - on campus - where you can present your research in front of a small audience. Interested?

1. Fill out this form to apply.
2. Our Graduate Student Representative, Joshua Durso-Finley, will get back to you with available time slots.
3. Show up and present. We'll handle all the logistics!

While we are open to students from all levels attending any university in Montreal, preference will be given to graduate students.

The setting for these talks will be informal and on an as-requested basis. Think of this as an opportunity to share what's going on in your lab, get feedback on your results, or encourage others to explore your field! You'll also become an official CONTRIBUTOR to the McGill AI Society.

Questions? Reach out to Joshua Durso-Finley with the subject line "McGill AI Research Talks" at joshua.durso-finley@mail.mcgill.ca.

Engineering Diversity at McGill Co-Chair Application

November 24
Involvement
Application

Engineering Diversity at McGill (EDM) is a one-day conference on equity, diversity, and inclusion, that was started by a group of EUS members in the 2017-2018 academic year. Now, with a year of experience, the goal is to expand the conference to a full day, rather than an evening, and to invite other Montreal-area schools to participate. We're looking for two chairs who will lead conference planning with the VP Student Life, and oversee a team of executives. If you're passionate about diversity in engineering and interested in event planning, consider applying here! For more information or any questions, please contact vpstudentlife@mcgilleus.ca.
