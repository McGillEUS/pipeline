EUS Sports' Broomball League Player and Referee Registration

January 16
Social
Registration

Looking for a fun and healthy way to relax between long hours of studying with some friends or wanting to meet new people? Hate Winter and can’t seem to find any good in it? Well look no further, EUS Sports is offering its annual Broomball league for engineers! Games will all be held on the rink at Lower Field and will last an hour every week. No previous knowledge of the game nor equipment is required. You can register as a team or as a free agent here.

We are also currently recruiting referees for this season, so if you’ve always liked to experience being a referee and/or you’d like to make a couple extra bucks on the side, please fill out this fill out this form!

Deadline to register for both is January 16.

You can also reach out to us at sports@mcgilleus.ca if you have any questions!

 

Winter Iron Ring Tabling

January 22
Academic
Event

Calling all Winter graduates. The Winter Iron Ring Ceremony will take place on Sunday March 10. Tabling for the Winter Iron Ring Ceremony will take place January 21-25 from 11:00-4:00 in the Committee Room. All graduates wishing to receive their iron ring MUST fill out the graduation package. Then they MUST come to ring fittings and pay $80 for their ring and reception tickets.

Fittings are as follows:
-Mechanical: Monday January 21st 11am-4pm
-ECSE: Tuesday January 22nd 11am-4pm
-Civil and Mining: Wednesday January 23rd 11am-4pm
-Chemical, Materials, and Bioresource: Thursday January 24th 11am-4pm
-Overflow (ONLY IF YOU ABSOLUTELY CANNOT MAKE YOUR TIME): Friday January 25th 11am-4pm

More information can be found at our facebook event our Facebook event.

 

Alternative Spring Break

March 1
Involvement
Event

Alternative Spring Break is an initiative taking place during McGill’s Reading Week that engages students with five days of volunteering, learning, and interacting with the local, Montreal community. Students will get to immerse themselves in social justice topics by working with local groups and organizations. The ASB experience is a reciprocal one and is there to benefit the needs and development of the student as well as the organization. Through this program, students can: make an impact in a Montreal community, learn first-hand about societal issues these organizations face, gain more understanding of citizenship and solidarity, build career experience. The application will close on Feb. 7th, apply now!

 

Apply for the McGill Dobson Cup!

January 16
Entrepreneurship
Competition

Applications for the McGill Dobson Cup 2019 powered by National Bank and hosted by the McGill Dobson Centre for Entrepreneurship are now open.

To apply, you'll have to fill out a form, and submit a 5-page business plan.

A few weeks ago, we raised $5M+ to power the Dobson Cup so that we could raise the bar and provide an even richer experience for students, researchers, and alumni that want to make an impact in the real world.

This week, we're happy to announce that applications are officially open. You have until Sunday, January 16, 2019 at 11:59PM.

 

Skating with QE

January 16
Social
Event

We’re back! Come join us on Wednesday January 16th for skating! Vacation may be over, and school may have started, but that doesn’t mean you can’t have a little fun with QE. This is a great way to kick of the year with some awesome people. We will begin by meeting for a little social at 64 Rue Saint Paul Ouest appt #304 at 6pm and then head over to the rink at 7pm.

The rink will be in the Old Port; Bassin Natrel, Montréal, QC. Bring your skates if you have them, and if not, for the first 15 engineering students who sign up, we will cover the cost of skate rentals and skating, however, everyone is welcome! Bring your friends! We look forward to seeing you there. For more information, or to let us know you are coming, please see the event page, or email us at queer.engineer@mcgilleus.ca.
