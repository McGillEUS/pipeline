EUSF Excess Fund

October 13
Academic
Application

The application for the EUSF (Engineering Undergraduate Support Fund) Excess Fund is officially open for the first time! This fund was created this summer in order to reduce the current surplus in the EUSF account.

This Excess Fund was made to give you the financial support required to explore your educational passions. Thus, if you have an idea for a new project, then we strongly encourage you to apply to this fund. You are eligible to apply to this fund if you are an EUS member AND the amount requested is used for Human Resources ONLY.

Here is the EUSF Excess Fund Proposal Form.

Please note that for a proposal to be considered for approval, signatures of the applicant, the department Administrative Assistant (if applicant is from Faculty or a Department), and the EUS VP Student Life (if the applicant is from a club or a design team), are required.

Once filled out, email your proposal to vpacademic@mcgilleus.ca. The proposal will then be reviewed and submitted to the Board of Governors by the VP Academic of the EUS and you will be contacted to present your proposal during an upcoming Board meeting.

If you are interested in submitting a proposal or have any additional EUSF related questions, please feel free to email vpacademic@mcgilleus.ca.

Montreal Space Symposium 2018

October 18
Networkng + Industry Info
Event

The Montreal Space Symposium is a public conference to discuss, address and plan on the current and future state of the space industry in Montreal and Canada. MSS will explore current and future issues within these fields while also highlighting recent advancements and contributions of the Canadian Space Industry and Space Research.

The symposium's theme "Bringing Space down to Earth" encompasses our vision of a united space program inclusive of all people. With this, MSS hopes to provide a platform to bring space more accessible to everyone, so that the space industry could be an accessible playground where all space enthusiasts of any discipline can have an important role to play.

Check out the Facebook event and register now!

NOBE x MCA Present: NOBE Business Case Competition (NBCC)

November 17
Involvement
Competition

McGill Engineering and Management Students! The NOBE Business Case Competition is back for another year. Partnering with MCA, NBCC is a great opportunity to get together with 3 friends and compete in a case cracking competition. You will develop valuable real world case-cracking and networking skills, while receiving feedback from industry professionals on your work.

Teams of management and engineering students will have three hours to prepare a creative solution to a business case. Teams will then present their case to judges, who are professionals from various sponsors (TBC). In addition to gaining case cracking experience, you'll have the opportunity to network and make new connections with industry professionals.

The winning team will also take home a CASH PRIZE.

No experience is necessary for this event and first-timers are encouraged to come out and get a taste of what case competitions are all about. There will be a workshop on how to crack cases before the competition kicks off.

DETAILS
- There will be 20 teams, 4 will be composed entirely of management students and the other 16 teams can have max 1 management student.
- Cost is $15/per competitor and includes breakfast, lunch, coffee, SWAG and maybe more...
- You can register as a team (4 per team) or as an individual and we will find you a team.
- Spots are limited! Your team's spot isn't reserved until everyone has paid.

Keep an eye on our Facebook page in the coming weeks for the registration link.

Become a McGill Biomedical Engineering Club Member!
Involvement
Registration

The McGill Biomedical Engineering Club (MBEC) is a newly formed student-run club with the objective of fostering and expanding interests in the biomedical field among McGill students. MBEC seeks to bridge the relationship between researchers among different engineering and non-engineering faculties at McGill University, by acting as a platform where members can collaborate on interdisciplinary projects in the biomedical field. It’s also a platform that offers students a comprehensive view of the field through hosting sessions for senior members, industry speakers, and specialists to present their projects and work experience.

If you’re interested in joining and participating in our upcoming events, please like the MBEC Facebook page and sign up to our newsletter via this link.
