EUSF Excess Fund

Academic
Application

The application for the EUSF (Engineering Undergraduate Support Fund) Excess Fund is officially open for the first time! This fund was created this summer in order to reduce the current surplus in the EUSF account.

This Excess Fund was made to give you the financial support required to explore your educational passions. Thus, if you have an idea for a new project, then we strongly encourage you to apply to this fund. You are eligible to apply to this fund if you are an EUS member AND the amount requested is used for Human Resources ONLY.

Here is the EUSF Excess Fund Proposal Form.

Please note that for a proposal to be considered for approval, signatures of the applicant, the department Administrative Assistant (if applicant is from Faculty or a Department), and the EUS VP Student Life (if the applicant is from a club or a design team), are required.

Once filled out, email your proposal to vpacademic@mcgilleus.ca. The proposal will then be reviewed and submitted to the Board of Governors by the VP Academic of the EUS and you will be contacted to present your proposal during an upcoming Board meeting.

If you are interested in submitting a proposal or have any additional EUSF related questions, please feel free to email vpacademic@mcgilleus.ca.

Hult Prize at McGill

December 1
Entrepreneurship
Competition

The Hult Prize is a social entrepreneurship competition that happens yearly at McGill. It was invented to inspire people of the university level to change the world through business. Yearly, teams of 3 to 4 people compete with the opportunity to go to regionals and then possibly abroad. This year the competition is happening December 1, 2018 with location TBD. The challenge is to create jobs for 10,000 jobs for youth. To find team members please join the facebook group: Hult prize at Mcgill- Matchmaking and like the facebook page Hult Prize at McGill- Competition for more updates! Deadline to sign up a team is November 20th!

Become a McGill Biomedical Engineering Club Member!
Involvement
Registration

The McGill Biomedical Engineering Club (MBEC) is a newly formed student-run club with the objective of fostering and expanding interests in the biomedical field among McGill students. MBEC seeks to bridge the relationship between researchers among different engineering and non-engineering faculties at McGill University, by acting as a platform where members can collaborate on interdisciplinary projects in the biomedical field. It’s also a platform that offers students a comprehensive view of the field through hosting sessions for senior members, industry speakers, and specialists to present their projects and work experience.

If you’re interested in joining and participating in our upcoming events, please like the MBEC Facebook page and sign up to our newsletter via this link.
