EUS Referendum Period begins Today

March 22
Important

The EUS Referendum campaign period lasts from March 13th to March 21st.
Voting wills take place March 22nd to March 24th at 5pm.

This year, there four main proposals that require YOUR approval

  • Restructuring of the EUS through the Governance Renewal Proposal
  • Renewal of the Engineering Undergraduate Support Fund
  • Revamping of the VP Clubs and Admin mandate
  • Ratifiying the creation of the Bioengineering Undergraduate Student Society as a new Departmental Society

More information will be available on Wednesday at referenda.mcgilleus.ca

For any questions regarding the referendum, please send an email to cro@mcgilleus.ca

NOBE Business Case Competition

March 19
Event
Occupational

NOBE is back with this year's edition of the NOBE Business Case Competition (NBCC), sponsored by Bain & Company. Grab three teammates and come compete in McGill's only case cracking competition exclusively for engineering students. Teams will be given three hours to prepare a creative solution to a business case, then will have the opportunity to present for judges. Our judges this year will be representing Bain, IBM, McKinsey, Accenture and more! In addition to gaining case cracking experience, you'll have the opportunity to network and make new connections.

No experience is necessary - there will be a workshop on how to crack cases before the competition kicks off.

Mental Tools To Help You Master Tough Subjects

March 24
Event
Skills

Most of us are unaware of best practices in learning—particularly in the STEM disciplines. In this talk Dr Barbara Oakley walks us through how to learn effectively, drawing on her extensive experience as an engineering professor and research and her work as co-lead on Coursera–UC San Diego’s Learning How to Learn, one of the world’s most popular MOOCs, (massive open online courses), with 1.6 million registered students.

  • The study techniques that help—and those that don’t
  • Why recall and chunking work
  • Using the brain’s different learning modes to best effect
  • Procrastination—aka “the pain in your brain”—and the Pomodoro technique

March 24th, 2017 10:00 – 11:30 am, OR 2:30 – 4:00 in FDA5

Refreshments will be served. To register please email maria.orjuela-laverde@mcgill.ca and specify which section -you would like to attend.
