TechTalk: Demos and Details of Research in Weather Satellites

Bob Rabin studied meteorology at McGill and later in France; he works at National Severe Storms Laboratory (NSSL) where his research focuses on uses of Doppler radar. Recently, he has worked to develop GOES-R, the next generation of NOAA/NASA geostationary weather satellites. The GOES-R series satellites will provide continuous imagery and atmospheric measurements of Earth’s Western Hemisphere, total lightning data, and space weather monitoring to provide critical atmospheric, hydrologic, oceanic, climatic, solar and space data.
Come hear Bob speak about working on the GOES-R weather satellite, see simulations and demos, and get to know more about working in the field!
When: Monday, January 9, 2017, 12:00 pm – 1:00 pm
Where: FDA 211 (Adams Building)
Pizza will be served.
