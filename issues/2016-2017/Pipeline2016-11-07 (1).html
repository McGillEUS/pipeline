The Journal of Paris, 1784

  A letter from Benjamin Franklin:

MESSIEURS,
 You often entertain us with accounts of new discoveries. Permit me to communicate to the public, through your paper, one that has lately been made by myself, and which I conceive may be of great utility.
 I was the other evening in a grand company, where the new lamp of Messrs. Quinquet and Lange was introduced, and much admired for its splendour; but a general inquiry was made, whether the oil it consumed was not in proportion to the light it afforded, in which case there would be no saving in the use of it. No one present could satisfy us in that point, which all agreed ought to be known, it being a very desirable thing to lessen, if possible, the expense of lighting our apartments, when every other article of family expense was so much augmented.
 I was pleased to see this general concern for economy, for I love economy exceedingly.

Robogals is Recruiting

November 21
Application
Involvement

Robogals is an international, student-run organization that aims to increase the number of girls and women studying engineering, technology, and science. We do this by engaging girls in these topics from a young age with school visits and other fun activities using LEGO NXT robots. Robogals North America is looking to expand to more universities this year, including McGill!

Six undergraduate or graduate women will form the executive committee for the chapter. They will join other women from participating universities and attend the Robogals SINE (Seminars Inducting New Executives) in January 2017.

If you would like to help start the Robogals chapter at McGill, please apply for the position of your choice here before the 21th of November.

Peace by Peace Volunteer Opportunity

November 9
Event
Volunteering

Peace by PEACE Montreal is a student-run volunteer organization based out of McGill University. Our volunteers work in elementary school classrooms around Montreal and facilitate workshops on issues such as conflict resolution, inter-cultural harmony, and inner power. Through games, role-play, activities and discussions, students are introduced to cooperative problem-solving skills in a fun and interactive environment. At the end of the in-class component, all of our students celebrate their accomplishments together at our annual Peace Festival.

Volunteering is one hour a week during the winter semester You can attend our open information sessions in the Education Lounge (in the basement) on Wednesday November 9th from 4-6PM.

If you cannot attend the information session and are interested in volunteering, please email peacebypeacemontreal@gmail.com as soon as you're able. You can find further details on our website.
