EUS Mental Health Workshop

November 25
Workshop
Health

With finals on the horizon, it is a good time to think about your health; while the last weeks of school may be tough, the EUS Mental Health Committee is here to help provide the resources needed to handle the situation!

We will be presenting a workshop Friday, November 25th in FDA 5 at 12:30 till 1:30, lunch will be provided.

This workshop will be going over the basics of mental health, the resources on campus that can help, as well as self-care methods for dealing with mental strain. We will also be going over how to help a friend who may be having a hard time. Hope to see you there ;)
 

TeN Screens are Active!

Announcement

Are you looking for new ways to get in touch with the engineering undergraduates? The Engineering Network (TeN) is a digital signage system used to display slides on a collection of displays throughout the engineering buildings at McGill. After much tinkering the system is operational again, now with more features! Don’t hesitate to e-mail ten@mcgilleus.ca for any questions regarding the service.

Submission Guidelines:
All submission must be in a 16:10 aspect ratio and can be displayed for a maximum of 30 days. Please specify your start date and end date in your message. Content irrerlevant to the EUS will not be displayed.
Please submit your content in .jpg or .png format to ten@mcgilleus.ca.

2017 CFES Congress Delegation Application

November 18
Application
Conference

Want to meet other students from across Canada in an awesome conference environment? The EUS is sending a delegation, all expenses paid, to the CFES Congress 2017, and is looking for motivated students to join the team. Congress is a weeklong conference from January 2nd- 8th filled with leadership, entrepreneurship and professional development workshops. Sustainability Applied Research Grant!

December 2
Funding
Sustainability

Calling all McGill engineers! Did you know you can get funding to conduct your own research on sustainability at McGill? It’s called Sustainability Applied Student Research—an opportunity for students to address the needs of the McGill community for academic credit in a hands-on way; bridging the gap between theories taught in the classroom and their practical application. For this round of funding, we’re looking for research projects that would explore waste management at McGill. Learn more about the ASR process and apply by December 2nd!
