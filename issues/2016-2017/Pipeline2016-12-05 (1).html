Faculty of Engineering Website - Feedback Needed

Feedback

The Faculty of Engineering is taking an initiative to re-evaluate the functionality and effectiveness of the mcgill.ca/engineering website. Our first step is conducting ‘Usability Testing’ which allows us to study the discrepancies between expected and actual user behaviour. We will ask volunteers to navigate through the website for specific information while narrating their thought process; their onscreen actions and narration will be recorded and analyzed. The data will used to formulate a plan for continuous improvement of the website.
 

Sustainable Urban Communities

October 30
Application
Sustainability
Academic

Sustainable Urban Communities, FACC 511: Selected Topics in the Faculty of Engineering, a TISED course, is being offered in Winter 2017! Instructor's approval is required and course is open to U3+ and graduate students in the Faculty of Engineering.You’ll examine the application of energy analysis in re-designed and re-developed plans for sustainable urban communities with a distinct focus on energy and materials use and savings. You’ll consider emissions reductions from the building sector as well as transport (e.g. electrification of transportation) towards developing sustainable urban environments. A critical element we’ll give to you will be an overview on the best commercial off-the-shelf technologies (COTS) for energy management and emissions reductions. You’ll discuss physical, economical and societal constraints and the effects of various design strategies on life-cycle impacts on sustainability of urban environment. 
To learn more and express your interest, visit TISED's webpage for the Sustainable Urban Communities course! Capacity is limited.

Questions? Email Lauren Penney, TISED Manager lauren.penney@mcgill.ca
