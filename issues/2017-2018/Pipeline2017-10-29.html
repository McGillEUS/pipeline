EUS Fall 2017 Referendum Results
Important

First off, a HUGE thank you to all voters! A secondary thank you to all the people who shared/liked the copious amounts of referendum posts. Every EUS member has their own distinct network of individuals, and the more people who spread the word within their network, the more members of the EUS can be informed and have their voices heard. The total voter turnout was 992/3303, for a percentage of 30.03%.

EUS General Assembly Fall 2017
Nov 8
Important

The EUS General Assembly (GA) Fall 2017 will be held in McConnell Engineering 304 on Wednesday, November 8th at 6 pm. The EUS General Assembly is an opportunity for all regular members of the EUS to bring forward motions, decide on the direction of their student society, learn about the EUS, and participate in direct democracy!


The General Assembly grants the EUS its legitimacy as the representative body of engineering undergraduate students at McGill and is empowered to take any action within the EUS including overruling actions of the EUS Council and the Executive.


The assembly already has many items on its agenda, however your patience will be rewarded by pizza and a sense of satisfaction that your student society is well managed.


At this time the following motions are on the agenda, and can be found here.


1. Ratification of Financial Bylaws
2. Ratification of Student Space Fund Bylaws
3. Ratification of Clubs Fund Bylaws
4. Ratification of Design Team Fund Bylaws
5. Ratification of Clubs and Design Team Bylaws
6. Ratification of Electoral and Referendum Bylaws
7. Ratification of Engineering Undergraduate Support Fund Bylaws
8. Ratification of Equipment Fund Bylaws
9. Amendment of EUS Letters Patent


If you want your motion to be added to the agenda before the assembly please email Speaker. Motions from the floor are also welcome.

EWB Junior Fellowship Application
Nov 4
Application
Involvement

SUMMER FELLOWSHIP OPPORTUNITY!!!

If you’re interested in international development and social change, this summer opportunity is for you!

Engineers Without Borders Canada is recruiting a bright individual like YOU for the 4 month summer Junior Fellowship Program. The program includes a summer placement in Sub-Saharan Africa with one of EWBs ventures. The application is open NOW and closes November 4th, 2017 at midnight. Placements are not engineering specific, so students from all faculties are encouraged to apply!

"EWB’s philosophy of fostering innovators applies on home soil as well as in Sub-Saharan Africa.

The Junior Fellowship Program equips today’s elite university students with the skills to create a more promising future—both for fellows individually, and for the wider world around them.

The program is not just an overseas volunteer placement. It is 4 months of practical leadership and opportunity to address the root causes of poverty. It is a platform to empower tomorrow’s leaders to affect long-term, tangible change."

CALLING ALL SOCIAL ENTREPRENEURS, ENGINEERS, DEVELOPMENT STUDENTS, ENVIRO SCIENCE LOVERS, OR ANYONE WITH A PASSION FOR POSITIVE CHANGE

The Hult Prize is a global student competition with the incredible opportunity to win USD$1 Million to fund a start-up idea that addresses a critical social issue.

In 2013, a team of McGill students won the million dollar prize and launched the very successful Aspire Food Group to tackle world hunger.

Each year, we send one McGill team to compete in the regional finals and we think you should be on it!

Hult Prize @ McGill will be held on November 25th, 2017

This year’s challenge: Harnessing the power of energy to transform the lives of 10 million people!

StartProud: Networking event for LGBT students
Nov 1
Event

Fier départ | Start Proud MTL is celebrating its 1st anniversary with a social and networking event.

This special evening for LGBTQIA+ students and young professionals interested in the legal, financial, engineering and business communities will be held on the theme of “AI, diversity and social media: How can we position ourselves to be the leaders of this revolution“.

The Explore event will take place on Wednesday, November 1st from 5:30pm to 9pm at 1 Place Ville-Marie, 41st floor, Montréal (RBC office).

In a dynamic question-and-answer format with the public, 3 panelists will present their career path and discuss questions related to the major changes they see in their field, including issues around artificial intelligence, diversity and social media. The evening will continue with a networking session between guests and panelists. Hors d’oeuvre and wine will be served.

Bilingual event!
Business casual dress code.
Registration is free but mandatory. Book your place now.
