EUS Conference: Call for Presentations
August 28 / September 17
Event
Important

Hey EUS Volunteers!! On September 17th, we will be hosting our second annual EUS Conference. The purpose of the conference is to facilitate the transfer of a large amount of EUS-specific institutional knowledge to all departmental councils, committee chairs, and club chairs, all at once at the beginning of the year. The ultimate goal is to ensure everyone begins the upcoming year on the same foot. As we want to make the conference as high a quality as possible, we would like to open the floor and see what you think would be useful to learn more about. If you have an idea for a topic that you would like to be presented on at the conference, please fill out the following Google Form before August 28th. As always, thanks for being awesome and see you September 17th!

Recruiting Volunteer Mentors!
September 20
Application
Academic

If you're looking for a community volunteer experience and if you like working with youth, sign up for Homework Zone! Homework Zone is a mentorship program that brings McGill students to elementary schools in Montreal. You'll be working one on one with a student, for 3 hours a week (you pick the day!) including travel time.

At the end of the semester, you will have:
1. A letter from the Dean of Students
2. 30 Volunteer hours credited to you on McGill's myInvolvement site
3. Valuable community experience

APPLY NOW! Application deadline is September 20th.

 

4th BBME Symposium
September 29
Event
Academic

Date: September 29th 2017
Venue: Jeanne Timmins Amphitheater, Montreal Neurological Institute Building (MNI)
Schedule: 8:00-19:00


Our goal is to gather together students as well as academia and industry representatives to discuss progress in the Biological and Biomedical Engineering field. We will have speakers with various backgrounds giving talks and networking session with the participants.


Submit your research as a poster, or present it at the 3-Minute Thesis Competition (3MT). Join our panel about “How to market medical research: challenges bringing medical innovations to the public”.


For more information about the poster session or the 3MT, visit our website. Please signup here for the 3-Minute Thesis Competition and submit the abstract for the poster session here.


The deadlines for poster and thesis competition submission are Thursday 7th of September at 11:59PM (Eastern Daylight Savings Time). The organizing committee will notify successful applicants by e-mail no later than September 14th. If you have any questions or problems with your submission, please feel free to contact mcgill.bbmess@gmail.com.
