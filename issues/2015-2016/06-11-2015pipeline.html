EUS Equipment Fund up for Renewal!

Do you love not having to bring a laptop to campus because you have access to computers in engineering buildings? Do you like having the opportunity for hands-on labs in your courses? If you said yes to these questions, and many more like them, you should know about the Engineering Undergraduate Equipment Fund!

The Engineering Undergraduate Equipment Fund (“Equipment Fund”) is a fund from 1987 that provides undergraduate students with additional laboratory equipment, including, but not limited to: computers for labs; lab equipment for ECE and Mechanical; printers and plotters for Architecture; 3D Printers for all undergraduate students, etc; with the full list of equipment available here.

The existence of the Engineering Undergraduate Equipment Fund must be put to referendum once every four years, and that time has come. The Engineering Undergraduate Society (E.U.S.) Council would like to renew the Equipment Fund for all EUS members by renewing the non opt-outable fee of $50/student per semester for full-time students (9 credits or more), and $25/student per semester for part-time students (less than 9 credits).

Please note that a majority NO vote will lead to the termination of the fee and all investments by the E.U.S. into equipment for undergraduate students. Your active involvement in this referendum is incredibly important.

Please feel free to stop by the EUS Office (McConnell 7) if you have any questions, email any member of the EUS exec team, or check out the informational table in McConnell next week!

We will be hosting a town hall on Wednesday, November 11 at 5:30 pm in FDA 6B, so please join us if you have any questions or are interested in learning more about the Equipment Fund!

Voting will take place from Saturday, November 14 at 12:00 pm until Wednesday, November 18 at 5:00 pm.
