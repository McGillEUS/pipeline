Fill out your course evaluations!

Did you know that course evaluations results are made public, but only if the class response rate is higher than 30-35%?

Course Evaluations period is now open and will run until December 24th (or December 8th for the Mechanical and Civil Engineering departments).

This is your chance to provide feedback on the course, the professor and the TAs. Your voice does matter and instructors do take your feedback into account. Course evaluations are completely anonymous!

CFES is hiring translators!

The Canadian Federation of Engineering Students is looking for two bilingual translators to translate their official documents. The translators are eligible for a share of the Federation's $500 translation fund as compensation for their work. Prospective candidates must be mostly available from December 30th to January 8th to translate PowerPoint presentations, motions, and reports. Afterwards the translators will be asked to occasionally translate emails, social media posts, and other short texts, until March 31st.
 

Sunnybrook Research Prize: call for applicants

Each year, SRI hosts the Sunnybrook Research Prize competition for
third- and fourth-year undergraduate students in the physical sciences and engineering disciplines. The competition recognizes excellence in undergraduate research with a specific focus on biomedical research. Ten finalists will be selected to present their research projects to SRI scientists, staff and trainees. The winner will receive a $10,000 cash prize.

The deadline for this year's Sunnybrook Research Prize competition has been extended to December 4, 2015. Details about the the competition and how to apply can be found online here.
